## Situation

The February 2024 ALPHV/BlackCat ransomware attack on Change Healthcare — the largest healthcare payment processor in the US — remains the most consequential cyber incident in US healthcare history. The attack disrupted claims processing for 94% of US hospitals, pharmacies, and medical practices for weeks. UnitedHealth Group (parent) confirmed a $872M remediation cost in Q1 2024 with total impact estimated at $2.3B+. Patient data for an estimated 100–190 million individuals was exfiltrated. Change Healthcare systems returned to partial operation by Q2 2024, but full recovery is ongoing, regulatory investigations (HHS OCR, state AGs) are active, and a second threat actor — RansomHub — claimed to have separately acquired and is selling the same data set. Any organization connected to Change Healthcare's clearinghouse or payment rails has a residual exposure that has not been fully assessed industry-wide.
